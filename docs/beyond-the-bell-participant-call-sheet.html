What to Wear

Wear what you'd normally wear — your style, casual. We want you to look and feel like you. Just a few on-camera housekeeping items:

  • No visible logos or brand marks
  • No busy patterns or fine stripes (they flicker on camera)
  • Avoid pure bright white and pure black — mid-tones photograph best
  • Keep jewelry to what you'd actually wear — nothing that'll clang on a lav mic
  • Arrive camera-ready — no hair & makeup on set

Bring a backup shirt just in case.

What to Bring

  • Photo ID
  • Your real perspective — this is a conversation, not a script
  • A water bottle (water + light refreshments on set)
Release forms will be signed in person at check-in — nothing to fill out in advance.

Prep Ask — Come Ready to Talk About This

No scripts, no "right" answers. Sit with these before April 25 so the conversation hits deep when we're rolling.

On the work itself

  • Why are you still in this? What keeps you in the classroom when so many are being told to leave?
  • When did teaching stop being a job and become something bigger?
  • What's something you know about these kids that the system refuses to see?

On being a Black educator, specifically

  • What's the cost of being "the Black teacher" in your building — and what's the gift?
  • Did your own teachers get you? What did they get right, what did they get wrong?
  • What are you rewriting for your students that you wish someone had rewritten for you?

Hot takes we may put you on your feet for (agree / disagree)

  • "Schools today are doing right by Black boys."
  • "The most revolutionary thing a Black educator can do is stay in the classroom."
  • "AI in schools will help Black students more than it hurts them."
  • "Being a 'role model' is a trap."
  • "Black educators carry a weight white educators don't — and we shouldn't have to."

For the intimate moment (just you and the camera)

  • Tell me about the student you still think about.
  • Finish this: "If I could say one thing to every Black child walking into my school tomorrow…"
  • What does Beyond the Bell mean to you?

What to Expect on Set

  • 10:30 AM — Arrive, check in, sign release, final wardrobe look in the green room

  • Breakout moments — one-on-one with the camera, deeper reflection
